3 Prompts to Try Right Now

Prompt 01 of 12

Understanding What's Actually Happening in Your Body

"I was just diagnosed with Type 2 diabetes and my A1C is [insert your number]. Please explain in simple terms what this number means, what is currently happening in my body, and what the difference is between someone who has prediabetes versus Type 2. I have no medical background so please avoid jargon."
Why this works

Patients almost universally don't understand what their A1C number actually represents. By asking the AI to anchor its explanation to your specific number and compare it to adjacent diagnoses, you get a personalised explanation rather than a generic one. The "no jargon" instruction is critical â€” without it, AI defaults to clinical language that leaves patients more confused.

Prompt 02 of 12

Understanding Your Medications

"My doctor prescribed [medication name, e.g. Metformin 500mg twice daily]. Please explain: what this medication actually does in my body, why it was chosen over other options, what side effects I should genuinely watch for versus ones that are rare, and what I should do if I experience stomach upset in the first few weeks."
Why this works

Metformin GI side effects cause many newly diagnosed patients to stop taking their medication before giving it a chance to work. This prompt surfaces the critical clinical nuance that GI side effects are common and temporary â€” information that makes a real difference in medication adherence and that your pharmacist may not have volunteered.

Prompt 03 of 12

What Questions to Ask at Your Next Appointment

"I have a follow-up appointment with my doctor about my Type 2 diabetes diagnosis. My current A1C is [number], I am [age] years old, and I also have [any other conditions]. Please give me a specific list of the 10 most important questions I should ask, ordered by priority, including questions about my target A1C, how we'll track progress, warning signs, and whether my other conditions affect my diabetes management."
Why this works

Most patients go into follow-up appointments without a prepared question list and leave having only answered the doctor's questions rather than their own. This prompt creates a personalised agenda based on your specific situation. The instruction to order by priority ensures you ask the most important things first if you run out of time â€” which you will.

Common Questions

What Patients Ask Us

Can AI actually help me understand my diabetes diagnosis?
Yes, when used with the right prompts. The key difference is asking specific, structured questions rather than vague ones. These nurse-designed prompts are built around the exact gaps in understanding that patients have after diagnosis â€” the things that don't get covered in a 10-minute appointment. Generic AI answers are generic. A well-crafted prompt gets you a specific, actionable answer.
Will I need insulin for Type 2 diabetes?
Not necessarily at first. Type 2 is often managed initially with lifestyle changes and oral medications like Metformin. Whether insulin eventually becomes part of your treatment depends on how your body responds over time. Prompt 07 in this pack specifically helps you ask the right questions about your individual trajectory.
Is this a substitute for talking to my doctor?
No, and it's not meant to be. These prompts are designed to help you understand your diagnosis better so that when you do talk to your doctor, you're asking better questions and understanding the answers. Think of it as preparation and education, not replacement. Prompt 03 is specifically designed to build your doctor appointment question list.
Which AI should I use these prompts with?
These prompts work with ChatGPT (GPT-4 or better), Claude, and Gemini. We recommend ChatGPT or Claude for health topics as they tend to give the most detailed, nuanced responses. Always verify anything important with your healthcare provider.
